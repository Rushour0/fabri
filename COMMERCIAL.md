# fabri commercial licensing

fabri is distributed under the [Business Source License 1.1](LICENSE).
The BSL lets most people use fabri freely, while asking organizations
that have grown past a revenue threshold to support the project with a
commercial license. This page explains, in plain English, who needs one,
who doesn't, and how to get one.

If anything here disagrees with the LICENSE text, the LICENSE text wins.

## TL;DR

- **Free to use** if you are an individual, or an organization with
  **gross revenue ≤ US $1,000,000** in the last fiscal year (or trailing
  12 months).
- **Free to use** for personal projects, education, non-commercial work,
  and internal evaluation (up to 90 days) — at any revenue level.
- **Commercial license required** if your organization's gross revenue
  exceeds US $1,000,000, or if you embed fabri into a product or hosted
  service you sell to third parties (regardless of revenue).
- **Auto-converts to Apache 2.0** on **2030-06-23** (the Change Date).
  Every version released under BSL converts to Apache 2.0 on that date.

## Who needs a commercial license?

You need one if **any** of these is true:

1. Your organization's aggregate gross revenue (across all affiliates)
   exceeded **US $1,000,000** in its most recently completed fiscal year,
   *or* in the trailing 12 months as of the date you started using
   fabri — **and** you are using fabri in production.
2. You embed fabri (or a substantial portion of its functionality) into
   a product that you distribute, sell, or offer as a hosted service to
   third parties — for example, a "fabri-as-a-service" or a SaaS where
   fabri is the agent engine under the hood. The revenue threshold does
   not apply here; this case always requires a commercial license.

"Production use" means anything beyond an evaluation pilot — e.g.,
running fabri against real customer workloads, paying users, or in a
revenue-generating internal pipeline.

## Who does **not** need a commercial license?

- Individuals using fabri for personal, side-project, or educational
  use — even if you work for a large company in your day job.
- Open-source projects and academic research.
- Startups, small businesses, agencies, non-profits, and individuals
  whose organization is under the US $1,000,000 revenue threshold.
- Anyone doing an internal proof-of-concept or pilot, up to **90 days**.
- Anyone using a version of fabri released **before the BSL switch**
  (v0.4.6 and earlier remain Apache 2.0 — see "Prior versions" below).
- Anyone using *any* version of fabri **after its Change Date**
  (2030-06-23), under Apache 2.0.

## How to obtain a commercial license

Email **pataderushikesh@gmail.com** with:

- Your organization's legal name and country.
- A one-paragraph description of how you intend to use fabri.
- Approximate scale (rough number of internal users, runs/month, or
  hosted-product end-users).

You will receive a license proposal. Pricing is currently
**relationship-based**, not list-based, while we calibrate against real
use cases — expect a fast, founder-to-founder conversation rather than
an enterprise procurement cycle.

## Prior versions remain Apache 2.0

fabri versions **0.4.6 and earlier** were released under the Apache
License 2.0. Those releases are, and remain, Apache-licensed forever.
Versions 0.5.0 and 0.5.1 were withdrawn from PyPI prior to general
availability and were never adopted; v0.6.0 is the first BSL release.
If you depend on an Apache-era version (≤ 0.4.6), your rights under
Apache 2.0 are not affected.

## Change Date — automatic conversion to Apache 2.0

Each BSL-licensed version of fabri automatically converts to **Apache
License 2.0** on **2030-06-23**, regardless of what happens to the
project or the licensor. This is a binding part of the BSL itself — see
the LICENSE file. The intent is simple: BSL protects the project during
the years that matter; long-term, fabri belongs to its users.
